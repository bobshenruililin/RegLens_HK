# RC3 corrections

Corrections are reviewer-controlled changes to internal metadata, extracted
propositions, evidence links, and caveats. They are not a shortcut around source
policy.

## Principles

- Keep original source bytes immutable.
- Add corrected proposition revisions instead of editing model output in place.
- Preserve reviewer identity, timestamp, reason, and evidence span.
- Distinguish factual corrections from interpretation.
- Re-run schema, domain, and release-boundary checks before publication.

## Source and privacy limits

Corrections may reduce extraction error, but they do not create reuse permission
or complete de-identification. Do not move real MCHK/DCHK text, OCR output, or
review notes into the public site unless a future source policy explicitly allows
it.

## RC3 guardrails

- Public availability is not reuse permission.
- robots.txt is not a licence.
- MCHK remains internal-only for public visibility.
- DCHK records must retain the July 14, 2018 publication-coverage caveat.
- No public real release is approved in RC3.
- Student-research letters support internal research only and do not unlock Pages.
