# User research protocol (RC4)

This protocol is a template for five formative interviews. It is designed to
test product clarity, trust boundaries, and workflow fit before broadening the
real-corpus pilot.

## Study goals

- Validate whether the Studio vs Observatory split is understandable.
- Learn which research questions users expect RegLens to answer.
- Test whether synthetic/demo labels prevent over-interpretation.
- Identify trust, citation, export, and review needs before Core50 scale.

## Participant plan

Interview five users:

| Participant | Target profile | Primary focus |
|-------------|----------------|---------------|
| U1 | Legal researcher or law student | Citation trail and editorial taxonomy |
| U2 | Public-health or policy researcher | Aggregate questions and bias warnings |
| U3 | Clinician or professional-regulation observer | Terminology and sanction interpretation |
| U4 | Data/product reviewer | Search, filters, and report usefulness |
| U5 | Privacy/licensing reviewer | Public/private boundary and release gates |

Do not collect sensitive personal data from participants. Record consent and
store notes outside public artifacts.

## Interview script

### Opening (5 minutes)

1. Thank the participant and confirm consent to take notes.
2. Explain that RegLens is not legal advice and does not predict outcomes.
3. Explain that the current public demo is synthetic and that real corpus work is
   internal unless separately approved.

### Background (5 minutes)

1. What kinds of disciplinary materials do you currently read or analyze?
2. What is slow, risky, or repetitive in that workflow?
3. What would make you trust a structured extract?

### Task walkthrough (20 minutes)

Use only the synthetic Observatory demo.

1. Ask the participant to open the tour and describe the pipeline in their own
   words.
2. Ask them to search for a synthetic recordkeeping warning.
3. Ask them to inspect the synthetic decision detail and identify the evidence
   for charge, finding, sanction, and factor.
4. Ask them to open research questions and choose one that would be useful.
5. Ask what caveats they noticed and what caveats were missing.

### Studio/internal workflow discussion (15 minutes)

1. If you were reviewing a real decision internally, what would you need before
   accepting a proposition?
2. Where should uncertainty be shown?
3. Which fields are essential for a Core10 internal report?
4. What would make a public real release acceptable or unacceptable?

### Closing (5 minutes)

1. What should RegLens avoid doing?
2. What is the single most useful next improvement?
3. May we follow up after the Core10 internal loop?

## Synthesis template

After five interviews, summarize:

- repeated needs;
- points of confusion;
- public-site copy changes;
- Studio review/reporting requirements;
- go/no-go risks for Core50 scale;
- items requiring legal/privacy review.
