The Medical Council of Hong Kong (Synthetic)

DISCIPLINARY INQUIRY

Medical Registration Ordinance, Cap. 161

Case Reference: SYN-MCHK-2024-001

Defendant: Dr Jane Example (Reg. No.: M99999)

Date of hearing: 15 March 2024

Charge: That she, being a registered medical practitioner, failed to keep adequate clinical records in respect of Patient A, and in relation to the facts alleged was guilty of misconduct in a professional respect.

The charge is brought under the Medical Registration Ordinance and the Code of Professional Conduct.

Findings

Finding: The Council is satisfied that the charge is proved and that the Defendant is guilty of misconduct in a professional respect.

The Council applies the legal standard that conduct which falls short of the standard expected of a registered medical practitioner may constitute misconduct.

The Defendant has a clear disciplinary record and made an admission of the facts at the outset.

The Council has considered Dr Chan v. The Medical Council of Hong Kong as a cited authority on professional endorsement principles, by analogy only in this synthetic fixture.

Order

Order: The Defendant be served with a warning letter. The removal order is not required. No appeal is recorded in this synthetic fixture.

Costs: No order as to costs.
