# Product positioning (RC4)

## Mission

RegLens HK turns disciplinary decisions into structured, temporal, verifiable
research data while preserving source-policy, privacy, and editorial boundaries.
It is not legal advice and does not predict outcomes.

## Primary user

The primary user is an internal legal or policy researcher who needs to:

- inspect source-linked propositions;
- compare charges, findings, sanctions, factors, and authorities;
- record uncertainty;
- prepare evidence-backed internal research notes;
- avoid accidental republication of real source material.

Secondary users include reviewers, public-health researchers, and product/legal
approvers who need to understand readiness, risk, and public-release posture.

## Moat

RegLens is not differentiated by generic search. Its defensibility comes from:

1. **Evidence-linked structure** - propositions remain tied to page-bounded
   excerpts and review state.
2. **Temporal decision identity** - stable IDs, immutable runs, and source
   versioning preserve auditability.
3. **Editorial codebook** - charge/finding/sanction/factor/authority/takeaway
   coding turns heterogeneous decisions into comparable data.
4. **Policy-aware release boundary** - Studio can support internal research
   while Pages stays safe, public, and synthetic-only until approval.
5. **Human review workflow** - model or parser output cannot become public by
   default.

## Pages vs Studio

| Surface | Audience | Data posture | Jobs to be done |
|---------|----------|--------------|-----------------|
| Observatory / GitHub Pages (`apps/site`) | Public readers | Static, unauthenticated, checked publication release; currently synthetic demo only | Explain the pipeline, demo structured search, disclose caveats |
| RegLens Studio (`apps/studio`) | Internal reviewers/operators | Authenticated local/private surface with raw artifacts, review state, and real pilot data | Acquire, extract, review, correct, and research real decisions |

GitHub Pages is public. Do not treat it as a controlled research room. Real
corpus data stays in Studio/private storage until legal approval, source policy,
privacy review, and publication tooling all permit a public real release.

## Positioning statement

For researchers studying Hong Kong professional discipline, RegLens HK is an
evidence-linked research observatory and internal review system that converts
decisions into auditable structured data. Unlike generic document search or a
spreadsheet of copied judgments, RegLens separates public demo artifacts from
internal real-corpus work and keeps every publishable claim tied to review and
source policy.
